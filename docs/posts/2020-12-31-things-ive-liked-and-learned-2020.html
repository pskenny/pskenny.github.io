Things I've Liked And Learned: 2020

Posted on December 31, 2020

Inspired by Fogus’ “The best things and stuff…”

Programming/Tech/Math

  • REST
  • Turing machines
  • Uncomputability of numbers (see “Uncomputable Numbers” in Articles)
  • Public/private key cryptography

Videos

  • How To Speak by Patrick Winston. Great talk about how to speak effectively with tons of examples in the positive and negative.
  • Every Melody Has Been Copyrighted (and they’re all on this hard drive). Look at brute-forcing music and the implications on copyright on own permutations. What about text?

Other

  • The Fabian Strategy. Thanks 30 Rock.
  • Hofstadter’s Law: It always takes longer than you expect, even when you take into account Hofstadter’s Law.

Articles/Blog Posts

  • How To Understand Things.
  • Uncomputable Numbers. Great blog post
  • The Computer Scientist Who Can’t Stop Telling Stories. Interesting look how an interesting developer does. “I write an average of five new programs every week. Poets have to write poems. I have to write computer programs.”
  • Code is Engineering, Types are Science. Easy to understand view of reasoning related in programming.
  • Gresham’s Law: Why Bad Drives Out Good As Time Passes. A small bit on Gresham’s Law. Once I knew I couldn’t help but see it.
  • Reality has a surprising amount of detail by John Salvatier.

Books

  • Click Here To Kill Everybody by Bruce Schneier
  • Art & Fear by David Bayles and Ted Orland
  • I Am A Strange Loop by Douglas Hofstadter
  • Unrestricted Warfare by Qiao Liang and Wang Xiangsui
  • Meditations by Marcus Aurelius

Music

  • King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard
